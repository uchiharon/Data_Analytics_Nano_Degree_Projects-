Introduction¶

For this project, a TMDb movie dataset which was cleaned from the orginal data on Kaggle would be made use of. It contains information of about 10,000 movies collected from The Movies Database (TMDb), including users rating and revenues.

As stated in the note given, certains columns like 'cast' and 'genres', contains multiple values separated by pipe(|) charaters, therefore splitting them would be part of the cleaning process. Some odd characters are in the 'cast' but instrustions says they can be ignored and the final two columns ending with '_ ad' shows the budget and revenue for the movies in terms of 2010 dollars, accouting for inflation over time

Key Questions:

  • Which genres are most popular from year to year?
  • What kinds of properties are associated with movies that have high revenues?
  • What factors affect the movies average vote (rating)?

    Conclusions¶

    After carrying out my analysis on the TMBd Movies data set, the following conclusions where drawn from the data set;

  • The most popular movie Genre from year to year is Action. Other genres that were found to be popular too where Adventure and Drame. Music Genre was found to be least popular.
  • The properties associated with movies having high revenue are their;

    • Genre, which is mostly adventure

    • Duration (runtime group), which is usually long(113 - 877 minutes)

      The movies rating (vote average group) could be said to be related to the revenue, for movies with high rating has higher revenue

  • The movies vote count is affected by the movies genres and popularity

    • Note: The limitations in this analysis where out lier which could be found in the popularity and runtime. This in turning with have influence on the analysis when I was grouping some cells
